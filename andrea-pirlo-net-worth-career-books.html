Andrea Pirlo| Career

The Italian soccer player started his youth career with the Flero youth team. After spending several seasons at Flero, he joined the youth academy of the local club, Brescia, in 1994.

Meanwhile, after his impressive performance at the youth level, he debuted in Seria A for Brescia against Reggiana and became the youngest player to do so for Brescia.

Following his stellar display at Brescia, he got the attention of big clubs from Serie A and, on the course, joined Inter Milan in 1998.

But, he struggled to find a place on the first team and immediately got loaned to his former club, Brescia.

However, the breakthrough came in his career when he signed for AC Milan in 2001. He scored his first goal for AC Milan in 2002 against Parma.

At Milan, he got the perfect suited position for him under coach Carlo Ancelotti and has helped the club win many silverwares.

Further, he went on to win 2 UEFA Champions League, two Serie A titles, two UEFA Supercups, one FIFA Club World Cup with AC Milan.

At Milan, he made 284 appearances and scored 32 goals in total.

In 2011, he joined the rival club Juventus and continued to add more silverware to his name.

Being at Juventus, he helped the team win four consecutive Serie A titles, two Supercoppa Italiana titles, and one Coppa Italia.

While he made 119 appearances for Juventus and scored 16 goals in total.

Interestingly, he played over 493 games in Serie A before moving to Major Soccer League(MLS) in the USA with New York City Club.

Altogether, he played 60 matches for New York City club and scored 1 goal. Finally, he decided to hang his boots from his professional soccer career in 2018.

International Career

Pirlo has represented the national side at various age levels. He made his senior debut for the national side in 2002 against Azerbaijan in the Euro 2004 qualifying match.

He won the FIFA world cup 2006 with the national side. Altogether, he made 116 appearances for Italy and has scored 13 goals.

Coaching Career

In 2020, he received UEFA Pro License and later signed for Juventus for a two-year contract. But, sadly, he got sacked after one season.
