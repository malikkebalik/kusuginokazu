Do Jehovah Witnesses Celebrate Thanksgiving?

Holidays aren’t a big deal to Jehovah’s Witnesses. Most holidays and other events that honor people other than Jesus are not observed by Jehovah’s Witnesses. Holidays like Halloween, Valentine’s Day and Mother’s Day are included in this category as well.

They also don’t observe Christian holidays like Christmas and Easter because they think those celebrations have heathen roots. Because there is no evidence that Jesus was born on December 25 and because many Christmas traditions, such as decorating a tree with lights and exchanging gifts with family and friends have pagan origins, the group asserts that Christmas is not approved by God.

Thanksgiving is not a holiday observed by Jehovah’s Witnesses. They use the time to do more door-to-door evangelism instead. According to the reports, an elder at a Kingdom Hall in Jackson Heights, Queens, since it’s a holiday from the secular viewpoint people have time off so usually, families and individuals will put forth extra effort in the ministry to go door to door.

He claimed that during the holidays there was a dramatic increase in the number of witnesses from his church who canvassed the city. He further explained that eight to ten people would typically participate in the service during the week. But on holidays, it can easily double to 40. The Jackson Heights church’s witnesses cover 80 territories in Jackson Heights and Astoria. Three to four individual blocks make up each territory.

It is not in the practice of Jehovah’s Witnesses to mark the holidays or birthdays of other faiths or nations. Jesus Christ‘s death which occurs around the time of Easter and Passover is the only holiday observed by these people.
