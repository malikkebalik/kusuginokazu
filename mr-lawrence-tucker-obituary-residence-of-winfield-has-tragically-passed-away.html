Mr. Lawrence Tucker Obituary

Remembering the Life of Mr. Lawrence Tucker: A Beloved Community Leader and Family Man

On Sunday, November 26, 2023, the community of Winfield bid farewell to a remarkable individual. Mr. Lawrence Tucker, a beloved member of the community, peacefully passed away at the age of 80, leaving behind a legacy that will be cherished for generations to come. Join us as we celebrate the life of this extraordinary man and reflect on the profound impact he had on those around him.

Death and Background

The passing of Mr. Lawrence Tucker on November 26, 2023, marked the end of an era for the community of Winfield and his former hometown of Dora. Born on January 14, 1943, in Birmingham, Alabama, to Lee Mewborne and Martha Bingham Tucker, Mr. Tucker’s life was filled with remarkable achievements and contributions. Throughout his journey, he touched the lives of many through his unwavering dedication and commitment.

Family and Personal Life

Mr. Tucker’s legacy extends far beyond his professional accomplishments. He was a devoted family man, cherishing every moment spent with his beloved wife, Barbara, and their extended family. Together, they created a warm and nurturing home that served as a sanctuary for cherished memories. Mr. Tucker took immense pride and joy in his children, Mark Tucker, Tracie Sanford, Michael Langston, Matthew Langston, Annette McMillan, and son-in-law Mike Johnson. His siblings, including brothers Tim Tucker and Mewbourne Tucker, were a constant source of support and love.

In addition to his immediate family, Mr. Tucker was blessed with a close-knit church family, numerous grandchildren, great-grandchildren, nieces, and nephews. Their presence in his life brought him immeasurable happiness and fulfillment.

While Mr. Tucker experienced the pain of loss with the passing of his parents, sons Larry Tucker and Christopher Langston, daughter Anita Johnson, sister Charlotte Tucker Handley, and nephew Tad Tucker, their memories remained etched in his heart. Their absence serves as a reminder of the fragility of life and the importance of cherishing every precious moment.

As the community mourns the loss of Mr. Tucker, funeral services will be held on Tuesday, November 28, 2023, at 1:00 p.m. at the Dora First Baptist Church. Following the service, he will be laid to rest in the peaceful grounds of West Alabama Memorial Gardens in Gu-Win. The visitation will take place from 12 p.m. until 1:00 p.m., allowing friends and loved ones to pay their respects and honor the life of this remarkable individual.
